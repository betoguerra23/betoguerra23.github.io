What is yawning?

Yawning is an involuntary reflex used to control the oxygen and carbon dioxide levels in our blood. Stretching our lungs and lung tissue causes us to flex our muscles and joints, increase our heart rate, and feel more awake. Another health benefit of this involuntary response is the redistribution of a lubricating surfactant in the lung tissue that keeps our lungs from collapsing. Studies have shown that yawning is contagious!
